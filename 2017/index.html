Hi.

My name is Kevin.

I specialize in content and experiences designed for every screen. My work sits between interface design, front-end development, and 2d animation.

I’ve lead digital design on Deeplocal projects like Netflix Make it, ABC Family’s Freeform Launch, and the Benefit Cosmetics Brow Translator.

Before joining Deeplocal, I worked at MAYA, a design consultancy whose clients include StoryCorps, Mondelez International, MSA, and Amway. There I also helped brand and launch AoT, an innovation accelerator for startups.

StoryCorps

StoryCorps.me empowers people to record and preserve meaningful conversations about their lives using StoryCorps’ proven method of facilitating interviews.

Our team was asked to help strategize, brand, design, and build the product for its Alpha release.

While the StoryCorps.me mobile app was being developed to record interviews, I focused on designing a web app to house the interview library, user profiles, and interviewing resources. I also worked on defining the visual language of the StoryCorps.me platform and how it related to the existing StoryCorps brand.

Before the Public Alpha, StoryCorps.me was named Listen.

Made at MAYA
My Role: Digital Design, Brand/Identity

ParkPGH

Our team recently completed a responsive redesign of the Pittsburgh Cultural Trust’s web app, ParkPGH—a site that gives users real time parking data for downtown garages.

I used the redesign as an opportunity to better structure the site’s information and develop a cleaner visual language.

Google
Doodle Wall CMS

Deeplocal invented Doodle Wall, a retail installation that invites Google store visitors to create digital graffiti on an interactive HD display. After the wall was installed in 5 locations (and counting) the Google Retail team needed a way to manage the content that was being generated.

I designed a simple CMS that allows Google Retail team members to filter, star, report, and delete doodles.

Made at Deeplocal
My Role: Digital Design

Netflix
Make It

Netflix Make It is a series of DIY projects that let you hack your Netflix experience. Our team developed, prototyped, and produced each of the projects—from the award-winning Netflix Socks to the more recent Netflix personal trainer.

I lead the digital component of the work, which ultimately meant the evolution of makeit.netflix.com. In designing the site, I created moments where we could introduce motion and produce interactive content that felt at home on the web.

Made at Deeplocal
My Role: Digital Design, HTML/SCSS, Art Direction
